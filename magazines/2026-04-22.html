5.1% Drop, 481 More Closures Last Year

Craft beer production slid to 21.9 million barrels in 2025 — a new post-pandemic low — with microbreweries taking the hardest hit at -8.9%, while taproom and brewpub formats proved far more resilient at -1.7% and -3.9% respectively. The silver lining: craft still holds 24.6% of total beer dollars, and breweries doubling as experiential destinations are weathering the storm far better than distribution-heavy peers.

⚡RELEVANT TO YOU — Taproom-first strategy is exactly what the data prescribes for small startups like Mayport — this validates the hospitality-forward model and quantifies the distribution risk.

Spirits RTDs Up 25.7%, Beer's Shelf Shrinks

Spirits-based RTDs grew 25.7% in 2025 and now represent 12.7% of all off-premise bev-alc dollar sales at $13.6 billion, with Cutwater and Surfside both posting triple-digit growth while retailers simultaneously rationalize beer and hard seltzer SKUs. For a craft brewer, this is the competitive redistribution happening at the shelf level — your cold box inches are directly at risk.

⚡RELEVANT TO YOU — Retailers culling beer SKUs to make room for spirits RTDs is a direct shelf-space threat in Florida off-premise accounts; understanding which RTD formats are winning is urgent for Mayport's distribution strategy.
