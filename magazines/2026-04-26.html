Craft Production Hits New Lows: -5.1%

The BA's annual report confirms craft beer's third consecutive year of decline, with production down 5.1% in 2025, 481 brewery closures, and the total count falling to 9,578 — the steepest drop outside the pandemic. The data also shows taprooms (-3.9%) outperforming microbreweries (-8.9%), signaling that Mayport's taproom-first model is the right bet in a brutal market.

⚡RELEVANT TO YOU — Taproom-focused breweries are outperforming distribution-heavy microbreweries in the decline data — directly validates Mayport's local hospitality strategy.

RTDs Hit $13.6B; Spirits Lead the Charge

RTDs now represent 12.7% of all off-premise bev-alc dollar sales, reaching $13.6 billion in 2025 per NIQ, with spirits-based RTDs up 25.7% and offsetting $650M in core spirits declines. For a small brewery, this is the clearest signal yet that adding a spirits-based or FMB RTD line isn't a distraction — it's survival math.

⚡RELEVANT TO YOU — RTD growth is cannibalizing beer shelf space; Mayport should evaluate an RTD sub-brand or co-packing partnership to capture dollars leaving the beer aisle.

Bev-Alc Sales Down; Beer Outperforms Peers

Total bev-alc off-premise dollar sales fell 1.9% YoY to $1.354 billion in the week ending April 12 per Circana, but beer's -1.2% decline outperformed the broader category, suggesting beer is losing share more slowly than spirits and wine in retail. Pre-gaming behavior driven by wallet pressure is also resurging — a taproom traffic driver worth building programming around this summer.
