Craft Beer's Three-Year Slide Deepens

The BA's annual production report confirms craft beer posted a 5.1% volume decline in 2025 — its third consecutive down year — with 481 brewery closures and total craft breweries falling to 9,578. The 60/39 split (decliners vs. growers) means roughly two-thirds of your peers are bleeding; the one-third growing are doing something right worth studying.

Foraged Botanicals Blur Beer-Wine Lines

Scotland's Fyne Ales is producing mixed-culture beers with foraged fruit, flowers, and herbs engineered to replicate the flavor and texture of specific wine styles — with no grapes involved. This 'wine chimera' technique is a differentiation play tailor-made for a Southeast taproom crowd fatigued by standard IPAs; local Florida botanicals (sea grape, elderflower, muscadine) could make a compelling regional angle.
