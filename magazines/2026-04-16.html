Craft hits historic floor — buy now?

The BA's official 2025 numbers are brutal: production down 5.1% to 21.9M barrels, total brewery count fell to 9,578, and 481 shops closed last year alone — the worst non-pandemic decline on record. The silver lining is the BA's own economist calling 2026 a potential 'buying the dip' moment, with equipment prices soft and lease rates collapsing in over-built markets.

Boston Beer owes $175M for cans

$ cat story.deck

A jury ruled in favor of Ardagh Metal Packaging USA against Boston Beer for allegedly failing to purchase contractual minimum volumes of aluminum cans from 2021–2025 — a $175.5 million hit filed in an SEC 8K. For small brewers, this is a loud warning: every volume-commitment clause in a can supply contract is a loaded gun.

⚡RELEVANT TO YOU — If you have or are negotiating any minimum-purchase clauses with can suppliers, packaging co-packers, or ingredient vendors, have your attorney review force-majeure and volume-flexibility language immediately.

Craft survival strategy: run in packs

BevNET's deep Q1 overview documents a fast-growing trend of craft breweries forming collectives and platforms to share production capacity, sales teams, and back-end efficiencies — from Frontier Beverage Collective (Coop Ale Works + Four Corners) to Abita's rebrand as Abita Beverage Group targeting 170K barrels. RTDs hit $13.6B — spirits lead charge

NIQ data shows RTDs now represent 12.7% of total bev-alc off-premise dollar sales at $13.6B, with spirits-based RTDs up 25.7% in 2025 and offsetting roughly $650M in straight-spirits declines; the distribution shakeout (Gallo, Pernod, Brown-Forman fleeing RNDC for Reyes) is rewiring which brands get cold box access. Tequila-based and Latino-positioned RTDs are called out explicitly as the next growth vector for 2026.

Beer prices spike hardest in March CPI

Beer led all bev-alc categories in year-over-year price increases in the March BLS Consumer Price Index report — a double-edged signal: it may reflect cost pass-throughs from aluminum tariffs and ingredient inflation, but it also risks accelerating trade-down to RTDs and value spirits. Watch your price-pack architecture heading into summer selling season.

⚡RELEVANT TO YOU — If your brewery is absorbing input cost increases, the CPI data gives you air cover to raise prices — but cross-reference against your local comp set before moving.
