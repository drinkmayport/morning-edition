Craft Beer's Worst Year — Now What?

The BA's official 2025 numbers are in: craft production fell 5.1% to 21.9M barrels, 481 breweries closed, and 60% of breweries reported output reductions — the steepest decline outside the pandemic. The silver lining: craft still outpaced the broader beer market (down 5.7%), held a 13.3% volume share, and BA economist Matt Gacioch sees 2026 as a potential 'buying the dip' moment for new entrants with differentiated offerings.

⚡RELEVANT TO YOU — Direct market context for Mayport Beer Company: microbreweries declined the hardest (-8.9%), but taprooms fared better (-3.9%). Experience-driven, locally rooted breweries are the survival playbook — exactly Mayport's model.

Boston Beer Hit With $175M Can Verdict

A federal jury awarded Ardagh Metal Packaging USA $175.5 million in damages after finding Boston Beer failed to purchase contractual minimum aluminum can volumes from 2021–2025. Boston Beer plans post-trial motions and an appeal, and says it has sufficient cash reserves to cover the liability without impacting operations — but the Q1 2026 earnings release on April 30 will show a massive one-time pre-tax charge.

⚡RELEVANT TO YOU — This is a cautionary tale about long-term can-supply contracts — a risk that applies to any growing craft brewery locking in packaging volume commitments. Review your can contracts carefully.

No-Fizz Beer: Texture Is the New Terroir

A wave of intentionally uncarbonated ales and adjacent drinks is putting greater focus on how texture plays on the palate, according to a new Craft Beer & Brewing deep-dive. RTDs Hit $13.6B — Spirits Lead Charge

RTDs across all segments now represent 12.7% of total bev-alc off-premise dollar sales, reaching $13.6 billion in 2025 per NIQ — with spirits-based RTDs up 25.7%, offsetting roughly $650 million in core spirits declines. Distribution power is rapidly reshuffling around RTD-capable networks, with major suppliers ditching RNDC for Reyes and beer distributors increasingly picking up spirits-RTD lines.

⚡RELEVANT TO YOU — Florida distributors are rapidly evolving into total-bev platforms. If Mayport Beer Company's distributor relationships are beer-only, now is the time to assess whether those partners are also picking up RTD lines — and how that shapes your shelf placement priority.

Beer Prices Surge — CPI Data Confirms It

Beer recorded the largest year-over-year price increases across all bev-alc categories in March, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics Consumer Price Index — driven in part by aluminum tariffs hitting domestic can-buyers harder than imported beer brands. The pricing divergence creates a complex environment for craft brewers: higher prices may support margin, but risk trading down by value-conscious consumers already under pressure.

⚡RELEVANT TO YOU — Tariff-driven can cost increases are a direct P&L line item for Mayport Beer Company. Aluminum tariffs are raising domestic packaging costs while leaving imported beer (like Modelo and Pacifico) largely unaffected — an unlevel playing field the BA is actively lobbying against.
