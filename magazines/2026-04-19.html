Craft Beer's Worst Year Since Pandemic

The BA's annual production report confirms craft volume fell 5.1% in 2025—its steepest drop outside of COVID—with 481 closures last year alone and only 300 new openings. The data cuts both ways for Mayport: painful proof the market is thinning, but the BA economist calls 2026 a potential 'buying the dip' moment for brewers who survive the shake-out.

⚡RELEVANT TO YOU — Direct competitive context: microbreweries were hit hardest (-8.9%) while taproom-anchored models like Mayport's held up better (-3.9%). Survival differentiation is in the model.

Light Lager Rewrites Craft Power Rankings

Sierra Nevada climbed past Boston Beer to #2 on the BA's top 50 list while celebrity light lager brands Garage Beer (Kelce brothers) and Outlaw Light debuted at #12 and #18 respectively—neither was on last year's list. The message is stark: volume in 2026 flows toward accessible, sessionable lagers, not complex IPAs, a consumer signal Jacksonville taprooms can act on immediately.

⚡RELEVANT TO YOU — The consumer shift toward approachable lagers is especially strong in Florida's heat-driven, tourist-facing market—Mayport's core lineup strategy should reflect this trend.
